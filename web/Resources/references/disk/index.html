Disk I/O Performance Parameters

The general timing of disk I/O consists of

  • Wait for device,
  • Wait for channel,
  • Seek time,
  • Rotational delay and
  • Data transfer.
Seek Time - the time taken to position te drives head system to the required track.
Rotation Delay - the time it takes for the beginning of the sector to reach the head.
Data Transfer - where the head reads or writes the required information.

We are ignoring waiting for the device and channel.  Assuming that this will be handled by the scheduler.

Calculating Seek Time

The linear formulae used is:
T = m * n + s

T - estimated seek time,
m - a drive specific constant,
n - tracks traversed, and
s - start-up time.

Rotational Delay

  This is dependent on the speed of rotation (rpm).  A floppy disk runs   at approximately 100-200 rpm and a hard drive starts at 3600 rpm (modern drives  are typically 5400 or 7200 rpm).  At 3600 rpm the disk passes the head every 16.7 ms, on average it will take 8.35 ms.

Transfer Time

The formulae used is:
T = b / (r * N)

T - transfer time,
b - number of bytes to transfer,
r - rotation speed in revolutions per second, and
N - number of bytes per track.
